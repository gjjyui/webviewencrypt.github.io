Schedule Blocks

An operator can use Blocks to schedule one-time Events or regularly occurring Events for the individual facilities that are included in the Block. Blocks can be placed within Blocks allowing users to organize scheduling to fit their own requirements. Schedule Blocks rank higher and take priority over any previously configured Enterprise Schedule hierarchy. This provides granular control over a facility's scheduled operations and reduces operational costs and errors.

Example

In this Schedule two Blocks have been established and contain:

  • Block 1 — South Wing and Pool Schedules.
  • Block 2 — South Wing, Staff, and Administration Schedules.

Block 1

Designed to control the Pool Schedule, Block 1 contains both the South Wing and Pool Schedule objects. This was done because a single Air Handling Unit (AHU) supplies the South Wing and the Pool areas of the building and the AHU is turned on by the South Wing Schedule object, not the Pool Schedule object. The hierarchical Schedule turns on the South Wing Schedule object and the Pool Schedule object at the same time but we need a way to turn on the South Wing Schedule if the Pool Schedule is on when the South Wing Schedule isn't. In RC-Studio, the Control-BASIC programming could be updated or simply use Blocks. In this case, the problem is solved by scheduling occupancy of the pool area by using the Block 1 Schedule.

Block 2

In Block 2, occupancy for the Staff and Administration rooms is scheduled but the AHU is controlled by the South Wing Schedule. A Block is created and set with the required Schedule objects that override the current hierarchy and resolves the issue.
